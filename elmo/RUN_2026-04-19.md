## Shared-output masked d=1 closes the unmask-cycle leak

A second masked variant, [`source/permnet_rm17_masked_d1_shared_output.c`](../source/permnet_rm17_masked_d1_shared_output.c) and ELMO harness [`elmo/elmo_masked_d1_shared_output.c`](elmo_masked_d1_shared_output.c), returns the codeword in shared form `(cw_share0, cw_share1)` with `cw_share0 XOR cw_share1 = E(m)` and performs no unmask XOR inside the encoder. ELMO triggers close *before* the caller reconstructs.

Results (256 traces each, same toolchain):

- **Peak per-bit signal**: `405.6` vs `3,779.6` unmasked PermNet, `3,794.5` reconstructed masked, `4,493.4` BIT0MASK. **11.1× reduction** over BIT0MASK; **9.4× reduction** over reconstructed masked.
- **Mean per-bit signal**: `204.6`, a **13.1× reduction** over BIT0MASK and **3.3× reduction** over reconstructed masked.
- **Leaking cycles**: `3 / 184 = 1.6%` vs BIT0MASK's 68% and unmasked PermNet's 38%.
- **Bit 6 signal**: `191.1`, down from `3,779.6` in the unmasked encoder — **19.8× reduction** on the specific bit that the paper's §5.5 flags. Bit 6 is no longer the dominant leaking bit; it now ranks fifth out of eight.

This directly confirms the analysis in `PROOF_NOTES.md`: the reconstructed-masked variant kept a high peak because its final XOR reconstructs `E(m)` inside the trigger window; holding the codeword in shared form across the encoder boundary eliminates that cycle. The cost is an API change: downstream HQC consumers must operate on two `cw[2]` halves rather than one.

**Honest caveat.** The remaining 405.6 peak and 1.6% leaking fraction in comparison 4 reflect the within-share-encoding leakage (stage-1 residuals on a pseudo-random share) that the d=1 probing model does not forbid. Reducing those further requires either multi-seed TVLA evaluation or a higher masking order (d=2).

## New finding for the masked d=1 composition (NOT in the paper)

The masked d=1 ELMO harness (`elmo/elmo_masked_d1.c`, one
pseudo-random share per trace) produces:

- **Peak per-bit signal** `3,794.5`, i.e. comparable to the unmasked
  PermNet-RM (`3,779.6`) and to the BIT0MASK bit-6 peak. The peak is
  driven by one cycle at bit 6, with `|r| = 0.997`.
- **Mean per-bit signal** `675.9`, comparable to unmasked PermNet
  (`586.9`) — no meaningful reduction.
- **Leaking-cycle count** `7 / 211 = 3.3%`, a substantial reduction
  vs unmasked PermNet at `38%` and BIT0MASK at `68%`.

Interpretation: the Boolean masking protects **within** each share
encoding (the probing-model guarantee holds), but the masked encoder
as currently written XORs the two share codewords together at the end
and returns a single reconstructed codeword. That final XOR and the
downstream `mov`s that write it to memory operate on the reconstructed
value and therefore leak the `m`-dependent bit-6 amplitude in the ELMO
Hamming-weight model. The surface shrinks to essentially that single
unmask cycle (hence the drop from 36 leaking cycles to 7) but the peak
amplitude does not fall.

This is a well-known masking pitfall: any circuit that unmasks before
its output destroys the probing-model guarantee on that output. A
proper d=1 masked encoder that preserves the guarantee at the
**output** would need to return two shares `(cw0, cw1)` of the
codeword and trust downstream HQC code to consume both halves in
shared form. That is a larger integration change, deferred.

The shared-output variant below is the design that keeps the
codeword in shared form across the encoder boundary and therefore
removes the unmask cycle from the measurement window. Numbers in the
first table above.

## Honest notes on the masked-d=1 harness

- Each of the 256 ELMO traces uses exactly one pseudo-random share
  drawn from a fixed-seed linear congruential PRNG inside the trigger
  window. This is sufficient to break the statistical coupling between
  `m` and per-cycle power in ELMO's Hamming-weight model **within the
  share encodings**, but does not protect the final unmask step.
- The LCG is non-cryptographic and exists only to make per-trace
  shares independent of the message for the ELMO measurement.
  Production code must draw each share from a cryptographic RNG —
  see `source/permnet_rm17_masked_d1.c`.

## Scope

Cortex-M0 is simulated in ELMO. The Jeon et al. attack targets an
STM32F303 (Cortex-M4). ELMO does not model M4. Reproducing the
paper's ELMO table says nothing direct about the attack on a
physical M4 device; that is Part 4.3 of the project roadmap and is
not yet done.
