## Honest comparison against paper §5.5

The paper's current wording is:

> PermNet-RM reduces the mean per-bit signal amplitude by a factor of 4.6
> and shrinks the leaking attack surface by a factor of 3.2 compared to
> BIT0MASK.
>
> The relatively high signal for bit 6 in PermNet has a specific cause.
> [...] the maximum per-bit signal (3,780) is dominated by one bit (bit 6)
> [...] The remaining message bits are significantly attenuated but not
> eliminated.

The mean-per-bit reduction in today's run is **1.4×**, not **4.6×**.
Bit 6 is indeed the dominant single bit (0.0135 out of 0.0135 for the
unmasked variant), but the *amplitude* ratio of bit-6 PermNet to bit-6
BIT0MASK is **0.0135 / 0.0230 = 59%**, not the **84%** the paper surfaces
in its "84% of the peak signal" phrasing.

Possible reasons for drift:

1. Different ELMO version or coefficient file than paper
   (the paper's Table 5 was taken from an earlier run; the current
   `sca-research/ELMO` HEAD commit is `7c4e293` but the upstream has seen
   updates that may have changed some numerical outputs).
2. `arm-none-eabi-gcc` 15.2.0 produces different Thumb code than whatever
   gcc version the paper's run used; ELMO's cycle-accurate model is
   sensitive to instruction ordering.
3. `-Os` vs some other optimisation level used at paper time.
4. The paper's published figures `3,780` / `587` are in different units
   than the signal-amplitude floats the current `analyze_traces.py`
   prints; the paper may be reporting unnormalised Hamming-weight sums
   while the analysis script normalises somewhere.

## New finding: masked d=1 on Cortex-M0 ELMO

Under this pipeline, the Boolean-masked d=1 composition produces the
largest empirical leakage reduction of any variant we have measured:

- `9.4×` drop in peak per-bit signal vs BIT0MASK.
- `5.8×` drop in mean per-bit signal vs BIT0MASK.
- Leaking-cycle fraction down from 64% (BIT0MASK) to 3% (masked d=1).
- Bit 6 is no longer the standout: per-bit signals are distributed across
  all eight message bits with no single bit exceeding `0.0024`.

This is a *single-trace-per-message* measurement with one pseudo-random
share per trace. It is not a TVLA-grade evaluation. The residual `3%`
leaking-cycle fraction probably reflects the PRNG-derived share leaking
within its own encoding half; adding multi-seed averaging would separate
share-specific leakage from message-specific leakage.
